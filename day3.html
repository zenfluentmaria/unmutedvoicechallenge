Your Reference Tool

The Trigger Map.
Fill This In Today.

This is not today's post — it is a reference you will return to throughout the full 30 days. Work through it honestly.

Part 1

When do I start shrinking?

The situations, rooms, and moments where a smaller version of you tends to take over.

e.g. Large group meetings / being put on the spot / speaking to authority figures
Part 2

Around whom?

Specific types of people or dynamics that reliably activate your pressure response.

e.g. Senior leaders / people I want to impress / native speakers / highly articulate people
Part 3

What kind of attention hits hardest?

The specific form of exposure that affects you most — not all pressure feels the same.

e.g. Being asked to justify / being challenged directly / being watched by someone senior
Part 4

What am I afraid will happen?

The fear underneath the behavior — what your system is actually trying to protect you from.

e.g. I will sound unintelligent / lose credibility / get stuck / confirm my own fear

You will use this map throughout the challenge. The more honest it is today — the sharper your interruptions become from Day 4 onward.

Part 5 of Your Trigger Map

What You Do
Without Deciding To.

The trigger fires. The fear activates. And then — automatically, without choosing — your behavior changes. This is the part most people never see clearly. Until now.

Over-explain
→
You give three answers when one would do. You are covering your back before anyone even questions you.
Rush
→
You speed up to get through the discomfort faster. The words come out — but the authority stays behind.
Lower your voice
→
You become quieter and less assertive mid-sentence. The ending trails off. The point lands softer than you meant it to.
Ask permission
→
"Does that make sense?" "Sorry, maybe this is wrong but—" Your tone goes up. Your certainty walks out.
Abandon the ending
→
You trail off instead of landing the point. You had it — then you let go of it right before the finish line.
Go vague
→
You stop being specific and start speaking in generalities. Safe ground — but it costs you credibility every time.
Trigger fires
→
Fear activates
→
Automatic behavior takes over
→
Now you can see all three. Now you can interrupt it.
